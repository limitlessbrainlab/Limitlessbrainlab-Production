The five brain types
Decades of brain imaging and qEEG research show that brains organize themselves into recognizable patterns —
distinct combinations of arousal, regulation and reactivity that shape personality, behavior and how people respond to
stress. Knowing your type isn't a label — it's a lens. It tells you which strategies will actually work for your brain.

    How we determined your type
         Strongly right-shifted frontal alpha asymmetry (−16.55) — associated with stress vigilance, emotional monitoring and "what could go
         wrong" thinking. A core marker of the Sentinel type (and of heightened emotional reactivity).

         Elevated arousal (1.58) with low relaxation (4.53) — the nervous system stays on, even at rest. This is the classic Sentinel signature of
         overactive vigilance circuitry.

         Weak stress regulation, severe burnout and low regeneration (22.3%) — this is what sets your profile apart: the Sentinel vigilance is here
         without a recovery buffer, paired with high emotional reactivity (Emotional Regulation 20%). That secondary Empath overlay is why pressure
         registers so strongly.

         Healthy cognition and alpha peak (10.50 Hz) — rules out a low-PFC pattern (Type 2 / Explorer). The capacity is intact; the issue is
         regulation and recovery.




     A word on brain types
     No type is "good" or "bad." Each comes with strengths and tendencies. The goal is not to change your type — it's to work with it.
